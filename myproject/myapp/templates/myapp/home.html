Our goal is to educate the public about the problems that arise from food waste and to provide effective mitigation strategies. We believe that reducing food waste is crucial to creating a more sustainable and equitable food system.

Did you know?

That approximately one-third of all food produced globally is wasted? This amounts to a staggering 1.3 billion tons of food that is lost or thrown away each year. This not only represents a significant economic cost, but it also has a major impact on the environment and contributes to food insecurity.

Reducing Food Waste at Home

Did you know that households are responsible for more than half of all food waste? By making a few simple changes to how you shop, store, and prepare food, you can reduce waste and save money. Here are some tips:

  • Plan meals ahead of time and make a shopping list to avoid buying more than you need

  • Store food properly to keep it fresh longer (e.g. use airtight containers, keep fruits and vegetables separate)

  • Use leftovers creatively to make new meals or snacks

  • Compost food scraps instead of throwing them in the trash

The Environmental Impact of Food Waste

Food waste has a significant impact on the environment, contributing to greenhouse gas emissions, water waste, and loss of biodiversity. Here are some facts:

  • Food waste generates an estimated 8% of global greenhouse gas emissions

  • 25% of freshwater used for agriculture is wasted on food that is never eaten

  • When food goes to waste, so does the land, energy, and resources used to produce it
